01 — The Big Idea

A plant is, at heart, a solar-powered sugar factory.

Photosynthesis is the process green plants, algae, and some bacteria use to convert light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose. It happens inside tiny green structures called chloroplasts, packed with a pigment called chlorophyll that gives leaves their colour — and gives them the ability to absorb light.

The genius of the process is that it builds something dense and useful (sugar) out of two of the cheapest, most abundant ingredients on Earth: the gas in the air and the water in the soil. Energy from the sun does the heavy lifting, snapping apart stable molecules and rebuilding them into fuel that almost every living thing eventually relies on.

02 — Where It Happens

Inside the chloroplast.

Sunlight strikes stacks of disc-shaped membranes called grana, suspended in a fluid called the stroma. These two zones host the two halves of photosynthesis — one chases the light, the other builds the sugar.

03 — The Two Stages

First catch the light. Then build the sugar.

i

Light-Dependent Reactions

In the thylakoid membranes · needs light

Chlorophyll absorbs sunlight and uses that energy to split water molecules apart — a step called photolysis.

  • Light energy excites electrons in chlorophyll
  • Water is split into hydrogen, electrons, and oxygen
  • Oxygen is released into the air as a by-product
  • Energy is captured as the carriers ATP and NADPH

YIELDS → ATP · NADPH · O₂

ii

The Calvin Cycle

In the stroma · light-independent

Using the energy carriers from stage one, the plant fixes carbon dioxide from the air into stable sugar — no direct light required.

  • CO₂ from the air is captured ("fixed") by an enzyme
  • ATP and NADPH power a chain of reactions
  • Carbon atoms are assembled into glucose
  • The cycle regenerates and runs again

YIELDS → Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆)

04 — What Speeds It Up or Slows It Down

Three dials control the rate.

☀️

Light Intensity

More light means more energy to drive the reactions — up to a point, after which the system saturates and levels off.

🌫️

Carbon Dioxide

CO₂ is the raw material for sugar. Raising its concentration boosts the rate until another factor becomes limiting.

🌡️

Temperature

Warmth speeds the enzymes along — but too much heat denatures them, and the whole process grinds to a halt.

05 — Why It Matters

More than a textbook diagram.

Photosynthesis produces nearly all the oxygen in our atmosphere, sits at the base of almost every food chain, and pulls billions of tonnes of carbon out of the air each year. Quietly, in every green leaf, it keeps the planet livable.
